FAQ
What does it cost?
Lacuna Stories is totally free
Will you have ads?
Never. Ads only distract from your experience of the site and its rich content.
How do you support this project?
Lacuna Stories has received generous support from Stanford University (for use in classrooms) and the general public (so that we can make it publicly available). If you would like to support our project or learn more about contributing, contact us. We'd love to hear from you!
Can I use the site now?
Until late 2014, we will be allowing a limited number of registered users, who we welcome to use the prototype of the site. If you would like to be considered, please contact us! The sooner we raise dedicated funds for the public version of our site, the sooner we can make Lacuna Stories fully functional and open to the world, free of charge.
Who owns the content I produce on the site?
You do. To better serve our users, we see what resources users find interesting, what kind of links they make, and how we can help our users more easily find the content that interests them. But at all times you control and own what you write, the work you do on Lacuna Stories.
Are there editors?
No. We don't want to edit or restrict your creativity. You're free to explore the site on your own, make your own annotations, and choose to keep them private or share them with other individuals, small groups, or share them with all users of the site.
Do you have an App?
No. We believe in the open Web rather than buggy and platform-specific apps. That said, you'll find that Lacuna Stories works on most smartphones or tablet devices.
Is this a conspiracy theory site?
This is a site for anyone interested in exploring complex historical events. Some may be interested in an academic study of primary sources. Others may want to examine particular pieces of primary evidenced. Some may want to read stories about 9/11, examine images, video, and other media. In other words, this is a site for everyone, and we strive to include content that is diverse, inclusive, and - most importantly - open access.
Why 9/11?
9/11 is the first of a number of topics that will be hosted on Lacuna Stories. Because of the vast and urgent public interest surrounding September 11, 2001, we decided to launch with this topic.
Can my group, university, or organization partner with Lacuna Stories?
Yes! We are already collaborating with different groups and organizations to broaden our content and host other complex topics on Lacuna Stories. Please use the contact form to let us know how we can work together.
What browser should I use?
We recommend Chrome, Safari, or Firefox.
Why the name "Lacuna Stories"?
Lacuna means a hole or gap, and refers to the important gaps that remain in our narrative of 9/11. Stories calls for a democratic inclusion of a diversity of narratives about 9/11, and recognizes that an official government report, a painting, and a novel are all equally valid and important attempts to understand and come to terms with such a complex, traumatic event.
